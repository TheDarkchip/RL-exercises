Level 3 observations: ensemble exploration compared to RND

The ensemble method produced a different exploration pressure from RND. RND
rewards states whose random-network embedding is still hard to predict, while
the ensemble method explores where the Q-value heads disagree. So RND is mainly
state-novelty driven, and the ensemble method is more decision-uncertainty
driven.

In the short LunarLander-v3 comparison, EDE-DQN did worse than RND-DQN by final
IQM, although it improved faster in the middle of the run. I would not treat
that as a strong performance claim because the run is small and high-variance.
The qualitative effect is still different from RND: the ensemble bonus is tied
to uncertainty about action values, so it explores where the value heads
disagree rather than where a random target embedding is hard to predict.
