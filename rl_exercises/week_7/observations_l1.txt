Level 1 observations: RND

In the short LunarLander-v3 comparison, RND-DQN ended slightly better than the
epsilon-greedy DQN baseline by final IQM. The run is still very short and noisy,
but LunarLander is a good test because useful behavior depends more on
discovering different landing trajectories and recovery states.

RND seems like a better fit for PPO than for DQN in this setup. PPO collects
fresh trajectories, so the novelty bonus describes the current policy's visits.
DQN stores the bonus in replay, so old transitions can keep old novelty values
even after the predictor has learned those states. That makes the intrinsic
reward less clean for off-policy learning.

The behavior snapshots support this intuition. RND visits different parts of
the lander position space over training, and the return improves late in the
short run. My conclusion is that RND is more useful here than on a dense-control
sanity check, but it still needs careful reward scaling because too much
intrinsic reward can distract from learning a stable landing policy.
